Gaming in Nepal



Gaming in Nepal is growing at a faster pace than ever before, providing lots of carrier opportunity to great players. But, it's clear that we Nepali people have been very vicious towards gaming in Nepal. We have always taken gaming as only a source of entertainment and to a certain amount it is, but we should also see the large growing industry of eSports (electronic sports) as it is taking the world's economic market by a huge blow. We are still backwards in the eSports scene because of lack of resources like fast Internet and powerful gaming machines but most importantly lack of support from parents in this sector. It's not wrong for the parents to be concerned about it if their children want to make a carrier in gaming or eSports as the scene is not well established yet in the country. But if we don't support a sector when it's in developing state can we just attach to it later?

The global video gaming business is growing enormously as the size of the market has been increasing substantially every year. The size of the market was USD 93.1 billion in 2015, which grew to USD 106.5 billion in 2016 and USD 121.7 billion in 2017. The projection for 2018 is USD 137.9 billion which is expected to reach a whopping USD 180.1 billion in 2021. Each successful game attributes to the growth of this ever increasing market. In recent years Fortnite the world's most popular game with over 150 million players worldwide had decided to organize a world cup gathering the best players from across all regions. They announced that they were going to giveaway 100 million dollars in just tournament prize alone. Large tournament prize pools started as Valve decided to organize a USD 1 million tournament for the game DOTA 2 (Defense of the Ancients) back in 2011. That alone brought talents from across the globe to compete in the tournament. As people started investing in eSports teams slowly eSports grew as an emerging carrier for talented youths.

Coming back to the country, we started out in cyber cafes playing games like DOTA, Counter Strike 1.6 or many other Single Player/Multiplayer games. The eSports trend flew in and small scale /local tournaments started in Nepal as well. Teams formed and slowly prize pools also went up in those tournaments and so did the craze. New gaming stations are being opened across the town bringing in talented gamers together.

In recent days PUBGM (Player Unknown's Battlegrounds: Mobile) has been emerging in popularity as it is widely available to play even in low end mobile phones. Other popular games that are emerging in popularity other than the classic eSports game like Counter Strike and DOTA are Battle Royale games like Fortnite, PUBG, and Apex Legends. They are yet to be well established in the scene but are growing in popularity through social media like Twitch.tv/YouTube gaming/Mixer. Content creation and game live streaming is being a huge part of the gaming industry as well. In fact most of them are more popular than the classic eSports players. Streaming has been so popular and made easy by cheap internet and mid end machines also being able to handle streaming and gaming. This has led to streaming being more competitive in the recent years.

Gaming companies have started to recognize the potential that the Nepali gaming scene has. WASD arena has been helping the scene in various ways like organizing tournaments and also streaming the games/tournaments, helping with servers in tournaments as well as online servers for players to practice together in. They have also recently opened an online store with gaming products for Nepali gamers in cheap price. I personally have bought gears from their shop and am a happy customer. The Warriors Esports (TW Esports) have been organizing online tournament in Faceit and are working together with WASD arena for organizing better tournaments here in Nepal. myRevenge e.V. a gaming company from Germany has recently taken up Counter Strike and DOTA teams from Nepal. Online tournaments like ESL India are also being played by many teams. Some have qualified for LAN events of ESL India Premiership.

On the other hand we have games with online tournaments like Fortnite and PUBG. Fortnite tournaments and scrims are really popular. Hamro Nepali Gaming had been organizing tournaments in Fortnite.PUBG M tournaments have also started to rise in popularity.

Only cyber cafes and small organizations used to organize eSports tournaments but in the past two years we have seen changes with big tournaments and popularity in YouTube gaming and streaming. Gaming Station like PP (Power-Play, Bhotahiti) which has the best players, who are helpful and always welcome new gamers happily. There are a lot others that we can name. But they are all bringing in the players together and help each other grow their gaming skills.

"Current state of Professional gaming is less about gaming and more about business growth. Unless government gives some attention to gaming field it's hard for gaming to gain bright future in Nepal. And yeah if Nepali gamers get some attention those people can obviously prove in International Platforms too" Says Nirmal 'Kamui San' Tamang, a Fortnite Player and YouTube streamer.

As a gamer myself I can relate to a lot of problems going on in the country regarding gaming. Also, recent ban of PUBG-M by government of Nepal brings threat to the eSports scene too.

We shall not just watch the negative side of it. Let's all of us stand together to let people realize the positive side of gaming. If we can be a step towards the success of eSports in the country then, why not?

Let's give it a shot. The gaming scene which is like a Fiction right now, let's all change it into a reality. Join us today!

Country in Fire as Government decides to ban PUBG M

All of a sudden The Kantipur Daily releases a news article "Popular online multiplayer game PUBG is banned in Nepal." Government has already sent out notice to all the Internet service providers. Slowly the ban will be effective in a few days. This incident has made the youth raging on social media and cussing the government. Let's dive a little deeper in this topic and evaluate the situation.

First let's discuss the things that have led us into this situation. It's obviously our parents who have filed the case against the game. This shows the generation gap that we have. I have seen videos of parents dancing after the ban. In their view they have done the right and are happy about the situation.

Alright the game is now banned, but is that really the solution? For the long term it's not.

Let me just put all of our views into this. The parents are also the ones responsible for what is going on. Too much of anything is bad. Gaming is not bad but unmanaged and unbalanced lifestyle caused by gaming is bad. Parents can ban a game but can't control the amount of hours their child plays a game? Thatâ€™s irony.

Random Nepali a YouTuber from Nepal brought up a really good point in his recent video about the PUBG ban. He said "Solution is not to ban the game but to manage the cause of its negative side." Click here to watch Random Nepali's video.

Should we not think about the people who were making a carrier out of the game; The YouTubers and Streamers? Are we all so childish that if we can't do a thing we just destroy it? Gaming progresses in other countries and we? We just go back and back with every wrong step we take. Should we question the government for protest? Didn't we try that during other problems as well? I'm definitely not saying that we shouldn't protest against it. But we know our government will hold us back or just stop like they do during a VIP "Sawari" I feel bad saying I am a citizen of a democratic country where if a single person does wrong then the right of all the other people is just snatched.

PUBG ban just threatens any other new games or any kind of entertainment sites to enter the country, killing the dreams of hundreds and thousands. The passion of people who really loved it will be lost forever. There might be alternatives but they aren't as effective. Seeing only the negative side is not the way to go. Have we ever seen the positives? Bringing in people together, creating carrier opportunities for many people in the eSports sector.

On the other hand, we might have been wrong in the sense that we were addicted to the game. That is certainly a bad sign and can have negative impacts on our mental as well and physical health. Isn't that why the game is banned? If people can balance life and gaming then we probably would not have been in this spot. There is a lesson to be learnt here though. We should also try to convince our parents and help them understand more about the eSports side and gaming in general. It's new to them so it's wrong to them. We have a part in it too. They will certainly understand us if we are able to convince them after all they are our parents.

Let's all of us do what we can to revert the ban. Let's protest in Social Medias and try to get to the government. Random Nepali also said in his video that if he has enough supporters he will file a case back against the ban. Go and show some support to a brother who is trying to help us out. Hope we can get the game back and learn from our mistakes.
